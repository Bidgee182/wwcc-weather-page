Overview & Data Sources

Where the information comes from and how often it updates

The Golfer page pulls live data from two sources and combines them with a 7-day history stored in the browser to produce all the readings, scores, and forecasts you see.

🌡️
Davis WeatherLink Station (On-Site, Wagga Wagga CC)
Provides live current conditions updated every 60 seconds: temperature, humidity, wind speed and direction, gusts, rainfall today, barometric pressure, and evapotranspiration (ET). This is your actual on-course weather - not a nearby airport or generic forecast.
🌐
Open-Meteo (Hourly & Daily Forecast)
A high-resolution forecast service providing 4-day hourly and daily forecasts: temperature, humidity, rain probability, UV index, wind, cloud cover, sunrise and sunset times. Updated every 30 minutes.
💾
On-Device 7-Day History
The browser stores a rolling 7-day record of daily ET and rainfall totals. This is what drives the Course Roll tile, Green Speed card, and wet course alerts. It updates automatically every hour from the Davis WeatherLink v2 API.
What RefreshesHow Often
Live conditions (temp, wind, rain, pressure)Every 60 seconds
All tiles, alerts, playability scoreEvery 15 seconds
Hourly & daily forecast (Open-Meteo)Every 30 minutes
Historical daily ET & rain (7-day store)Every 60 minutes
🔴
Stale Data Warning - If the "last updated" timestamp turns red, the connection to the Davis station has dropped and live values may be more than 10 minutes old. Forecast data (Open-Meteo) is unaffected.

Playing Conditions Banner

The overall playability rating shown at the top of the page

The large banner at the top of the Golfer page gives an instant overall rating of playing conditions. It is calculated by a penalty point system - the dashboard starts at zero and adds penalty points for each factor that makes conditions worse. The total score determines the rating.

Outstanding
Score: 0
Excellent
Score: 1
Good
Score: 2–3
Fair
Score: 4–5
Tough
Score: 6–7
Not Playable
Score: 8+

How Penalty Points Are Added

🌡️Feels Like Temp+3 pts if < 6°C or > 36°C (dangerously cold/hot) · +2 pts if < 10°C or > 32°C · +1 pt if < 14°C or > 28°C
💨Wind Speed+2 pts if > 40 km/h (very strong) · +1 pt if > 25 km/h
🌧️24-Hour Rain Total+4 pts if ≥ 30 mm · +3 pts if ≥ 15 mm · +2 pts if ≥ 5 mm · +1 pt if ≥ 0.5 mm
🌦️Forecast Rain (if no actual rain)+2 pts if > 70% probability · +1 pt if > 40% probability
☀️UV Index+2 pts if > 10 (extreme) · +1 pt if > 7 (very high)
☁️Cloud Cover+1 pt if > 85% overcast
🌅Daylight Quality+1 pt within 30 min of sunrise · +1 pt low morning light · +1–3 pts as sunset approaches · +3 pts after sunset
💧Course Condition+2 pts if waterlogged (7-day balance < −20 mm surplus) · +1 pt if wet (−5 to −20 mm surplus)
💦Humidity+2 pts if RH > 90% · +1 pt if RH > 80%
ℹ️
The banner also shows a short summary line: "Feels 24°C · Calm · No rain expected" - listing the key factors contributing to the current rating.

The 8 Condition Tiles

Each tile explained - what it shows, how it's calculated, and what the colours mean

Tile 1 - Feels Like Temperature

Not just the air temperature - this is how hot or cold it actually feels on your skin, accounting for wind chill and the humidity effect on heat dissipation. It uses the Australian Apparent Temperature (AT) formula:

e = (RH ÷ 100) × 6.105 × exp(17.27 × T ÷ (237.7 + T)) AT = T + (0.33 × e) − (0.70 × wind m/s) − 4.00 Where: T = air temperature (°C) RH = relative humidity (%) e = water vapour pressure AT = apparent / feels-like temperature (°C)

15–26°CGreenIdeal golfing temperature
26–30°CYellowWarm - keep the water bottle handy
30–35°COrangeHot - stay in the shade between holes
> 35°CRedDangerously hot - consider postponing

Tile 5 - Course Roll

Indicates how firm or soft the course is underfoot. Calculated from the 7-day water balance: total evapotranspiration (ET) minus total rainfall over the past 7 days.

7-Day Deficit = Sum(ET per day) − Sum(Rain per day) over rolling 7 days Positive = drying out (ET exceeds rain) Negative = surplus (rain exceeds ET)

0 to +15 mmNormalGreenPlaying true - ideal ball roll on fairways
+15 to +30 mmFirmYellowBall running on - allow extra run on approaches
> +30 mmVery FirmOrangeHard and fast - significant run; greens receptive

Tile 6 - 24-Hour Rain Total

Card 3 - Ball Flight at 150m

Shows how far the ball will actually fly in current atmospheric conditions compared to a standard 150m shot. Three factors affect carry distance:

Base distance = 150 m Temperature adj = (Air Temp − 20°C) × 0.20 m warmer = farther Pressure adj = (1015 − Pressure) × 0.03 m lower pressure = farther Humidity adj = (RH − 50%) × 0.005 m minor effect Playing distance = round(150 + temp adj + pressure adj + humidity adj)
Difference from 150mClub RecommendationColour
Within ±1 mNormal - no change neededGreen
+1 to +4 m longerClub down (ball flying farther)Blue
−1 to −4 m shorterClub up (ball flying shorter)Yellow
> +4 m longerClub downBlue
> −4 m shorterClub upOrange

The card also notes the specific contributing conditions - for example: "Warm air (+3m) · Low pressure (+1m) · Result: 4m farther than usual".

💡
Wind is not included in the ball distance calculation - it is too variable hole-to-hole. The current wind speed and direction is shown separately below the distance so golfers can factor it in themselves for each shot.

The dashboard scans the forecast for the window from 8 PM tonight to 9 AM tomorrow and finds the minimum expected temperature. This drives both the frost card and the frost alert banner.

Based on the current UV index, the card calculates the safe sun exposure time for a moderately fair-skinned golfer wearing SPF 30 sunscreen:

UV IndexLevelSafe Time (SPF 30)Colour
≤ 2LowNo limitGreen
3–5Moderate60 minutesYellow
6–7High30 minutesOrange

The card counts down the remaining safe time. When the timer reaches zero it shows "Reapply Now" - a reminder to reapply sunscreen immediately.

☀️
Wagga Wagga regularly records UV indices above 10 in summer. An 18-hole round takes 4+ hours - reapplication every 2 hours regardless of the timer is strongly recommended on high UV days.

Alert Banners

Automatic warnings shown above the page when specific thresholds are triggered

Alert banners appear automatically at the top of the Golfer page when specific conditions are forecast or observed. They disappear once the conditions pass.

⚡
Thunderstorm Alert
Trigger: WMO weather code 95, 96, or 99 (thunderstorm with or without hail) forecast in the next 4 hours.
Message: "Thunderstorm forecast in the next 3 hours - lightning risk in the area"
Action: Suspend play immediately if thunder is heard. The 30/30 rule applies: seek shelter if thunder follows lightning by less than 30 seconds; wait 30 minutes after last thunder before resuming.
❄️
Frost Alert
Trigger: Overnight forecast minimum below 4°C (8 PM – 9 AM window).
Below 2°C: "Frost likely overnight - delay early tee times, hold carts on paths until greens clear"
2–4°C: "Frost possible overnight - monitor greens before early tee times"
Action: Starter should check greens before first tee times. Walking on frosted bentgrass causes cell rupture and brown footprint damage.
🌫️
Fog Alert
Trigger: WMO weather code 45 (fog) or 48 (rime fog) forecast for tomorrow morning (up to noon).
Message: "Fog forecast tomorrow morning - check visibility before early tee times"
Action: If visibility is less than 50m, consider delaying tee times until fog lifts.
🛺
Carts on Paths Alert
Trigger: Either of these conditions:
 • 7-day water balance shows surplus > 10 mm (waterlogged soil)
 • 24-hour rainfall total ≥ 15 mm
Message: "Course wet - carts on paths only"
Action: Enforce path-only policy for all motorised carts to prevent turf damage on saturated ground.

Pressure Trend - The ↑ / → / ↓ arrow next to pressure is calculated from a 10-reading rolling buffer. Rising pressure (> +0.5 hPa/hr) generally indicates improving conditions.
